About the Center Hill Lake dataset: Includes closed single-family residential sales across major CHL lakefront subdivisions including Floating Mill Village, Harbor Pointe, Blue Water Bay, Hurricane Pointe, Casey Cove, Lakeview Mountain Estates, and others. Center Hill Lake (18,220 acres, 415 miles of shoreline) is the largest and deepest clear-water lake in Middle Tennessee, managed by the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers. The Corps permitting environment is more restrictive than TVA-managed Tims Ford, which is a key differentiator for dock buyers.

Budget Guide: Where Your Dollar Goes Farthest
CHL vs. TFL by price tier — based on 2025 market data

🎯 $500K–$750K — CHL Wins

Much more selection at CHL — Blue Water Bay, Hurricane Pointe mid-tier, The Falls at Center Hill. TFL at this range is mostly Twin Creeks townhomes and smaller homes without premium water access. The 2025 CHL median of $627,500 means you're shopping right at the heart of the market.

🦅 $750K–$1.2M — Both Competitive

Both lakes offer strong options here. CHL: Blue Water Bay upper tier, Harbor Pointe, Floating Mill Village. TFL: Barefoot Bay, Twin Creeks upper, entry-level Fanning Bend, Elklore, Cline Ridge. Decision comes down to drive tolerance and dock priority.

📈 Appreciation Leader: Center Hill

CHL posted +81.4% median appreciation over 6 years vs. TFL's +54.6%. The price gap between the two lakes has narrowed from 50% in 2020 to ~28% in 2025, suggesting CHL is repricing faster and may represent the stronger relative value for buyers entering today.

✅ $1.2M+ — Tims Ford Wins

TFL wins decisively in inventory quality and selection at the top of the market. Fanning Bend, Hopkins Point Bluff Dr, Highland Ridge, and Ridgeville Sub offer world-class lakefront with no comparable CHL offering — and TFL's average crossed $1M for the first time in 2025.
